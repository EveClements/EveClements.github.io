About Me

In 2015 whilst procrastinating revision for my degree finals I happened to see a house for sale online in Morocco and, seeing the potential implications this might have for further procrastinating opportunities, I suggested that evening to my then fiancé that perhaps we should consider moving to Africa. Following a swift first trip to Morocco, we shook hands with the vendor over some tapas and a glass of wine. And, with a small amount of paperwork and minimum of fuss, the deed was done†. In November that year, after the finals were over and some other minor things like our wedding were out the way, we packed our bindles and moved to Essaouira (the bindles took a while longer to arrive - apparently importing some mouldy old clothes and a moth-eaten teddy bear requires more paperwork than buying a house).


Currently we are thriving in the Moroccan culture, improving our poor high school French and picking up the local arabic dialect. I have been occupying myself with some remote learning courses and self study as well as a number of personal projects such as inventing recipes and collecting other content with the hope of launching a successful blog and/or book in the next few years. Whilst mooching off Adam is working out well, I’m starting to wonder if perhaps seeking some gainful employment of my own might be the way forward in life.
